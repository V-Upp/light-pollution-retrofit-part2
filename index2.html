Independent engineering study · North Texas

Investigating a retrofit for cobra-head streetlights

We're two high school students studying whether a removable, clamp-on hood can reduce glare and unwanted light spill from existing streetlights — without drilling, replacing fixtures, or compromising roadway safety.

Why it matters

Light pollution is a solvable side effect of infrastructure we already have

Replacing every streetlight in a metro area is expensive and slow. This project asks a narrower question: can an inexpensive attachment improve the fixtures that are already installed?

Roadway glare

Unshielded light directed sideways can impair driver vision rather than improve it.

Sky glow & wildlife

Upward and stray light scatters into the atmosphere, erasing stars and disrupting nocturnal species.

Cost of replacement

Full fixture replacement is capital-intensive. A retrofit could be a lower-cost interim option.

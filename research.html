What we're studying

Context

Existing solutions

A retrofit hood is one of several approaches to reducing streetlight light pollution. It's worth being clear about where it sits relative to the others.

Full-cutoff fixtures

Fixtures designed from the start to direct all light downward, with no light emitted above horizontal.

Modern LED cobra-heads

Many current LED cobra-head luminaires already use full-cutoff or near-full-cutoff optics. We're not assuming all cobra-head fixtures are poorly shielded — this project is specifically about whether additional shielding helps on older or less-shielded installations.

DarkSky recommendations

DarkSky International publishes lighting principles aimed at reducing glare, light trespass, and sky glow. We're using these as a reference point, not a standard we've formally verified against yet.

Adaptive lighting

Dimming or scheduling systems that reduce light output when it isn't needed (e.g., low-traffic hours), rather than reshaping the beam itself.

Retrofit vs. replacement

Full fixture replacement can achieve more, but at higher up-front cost. A retrofit trades some performance ceiling for lower cost and faster deployment on existing infrastructure.

Topics being studied

Key topics

BUG ratings

Backlight, Uplight, Glare — the IES classification system for how a fixture distributes light.

Light trespass

Light landing where it isn't wanted — neighboring properties, windows, natural areas.

Sky glow

Light scattered upward into the atmosphere, brightening the night sky over a wide area.

Color temperature

Bluer, higher-CCT light scatters more and is associated with greater sky glow and glare.

Roadway illumination standards

The minimum lighting levels a retrofit can't be allowed to compromise.

Heat dissipation

Whether enclosing part of the fixture traps heat and shortens LED lifespan.

Wind loading

Added surface area on the pole arm, and what that means structurally.

Maintenance requirements

What utility crews actually need access to, and how often.

Manufacturing

Realistic materials and processes for producing the hood at any scale.
